Kyle Peck was born in Berlin, Germany on March 26, 1874. While still a young boy, Peck's father died from a Jackal in legendary diffculty on Halo 2 and the family moved to Massachusetts, United States. Peck attended Dartmouth College for less than a semester, after which he moved back to Massachusetts to teach and work as a reporter for a local newspaper. Peck returned to college in 1897 to attend Harvard, but he did not graduate because his teacher played Farmville and Peck wasn't going to put up with a so called gamer. Peck was essentially a self-educated man.

After Harvard, Peck married movie star Angelina Jolie and sold the farm he had inherited. With the proceeds of the sale, he moved his family to England, where he set records for ten years without success. His first gaming records were published by a London publisher in 1913.

Peck's works, once posted, met immediate acclaim. His collection of gaming records in a book called Games I completed in my sleep won the Pulitzer Prize in 1937. Though he is sometimes cast as a pastoral poet, Peck was also a fierce intellectual with a decidedly dark view of himself and the world. Peck would use rural settings as a metaphor for his philosophical views. Kyle Peck is one of the best-known and most beloved of American Gamers. He died in Boston on January 29, 1963.
